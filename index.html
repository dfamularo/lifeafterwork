What will your life after work
look like?

Life after work means different things to different people. You might be looking forward to the trip of a lifetime, having the time to invest in a new hobby, or spending more time with those who really matter.

One thing is certain: we all need to make sure that our super can help us get there. But planning for retirement can be complicated, and if you’re like most people, it’s hard to know where to start when you don’t know what you don’t know.

With our help, people like you are making smart decisions to put them in the best position they can be for a great life after work.

Which of these stories sounds like you?

Still working full-time, but starting to plan for retirement?

Jane from Toowoomba is looking forward to her retirement, which is only a few years away. She wanted to make sure that she was doing everything she could to maximise her super in these last few years without impacting her current lifestyle.

With a little help, Jane worked out a way to increase the amount she pays into her super through salary sacrifice, without reducing the money she actually takes home every month. This means saving more, but effectively being paid the same.

Find out more about how you can increase your super balance, while still receiving the same take home pay every month.

Already retired and want flexibility to use your super balance as you want?

Marco’s dream was to travel around Australia when he retired. And that’s exactly what he did, while also growing his super. But how?

When he retired, Marco wanted to take money from his super to buy a caravan and see more of Australia. With a little help, he worked out how he could take out an initial cash lump sum, while still receiving regular monthly payments from his super.

Plus, Marco learned that if Marg also moved her super to iQ Retirement, they could link their accounts and pay lower fees.

Their combined super continued to grow while they were road tripping. It was the perfect solution for them.

Find out how you can increase the flexibility of your super to allow you to live the life after work you want.

Already finished working and want to make sure you've set up your retirement income in the best way?

Sixty-eight-year-old Pavel lives in Newcastle, NSW. When he retired, he thought about taking his super as a lump sum to put in his savings account. But he spoke to Russell Investments before doing anything, and we showed him how iQ Retirement could help his money last longer.

Now Pavel gets tax-free income every month and the rest of his super savings are still invested. It's as if he is being paid a salary, but without all the hard work.
